Abdul Wali grew up in an almond orchard outside Mazar-i-Sharif and studied
agricultural engineering in India. Sabz Farm assembled low-cost drip
irrigation kits from imported parts and sold them with a promise: call our
hotline and an agronomist answers in your language.

The kits worked — trial orchards cut water use by a third. The business
didn't. Farmers wanted to pay after harvest, suppliers wanted payment up
front, and no lender would bridge the gap. After two seasons of growing
almonds and shrinking cash, Abdul Wali paused the company and took a job
with an NGO's agriculture program.

He calls it a pause, not an end. The supplier contacts, the hotline scripts,
and the trial data are documented and waiting. "The orchards are still
there," he says. "So is the water problem."
