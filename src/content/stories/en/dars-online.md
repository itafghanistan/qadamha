> **Example story.** This profile is fictional, written to show what a Qadamha
> story looks like. Help us replace it with a real one.

Zahra was a physics teacher in Herat who kept meeting bright students from
districts where schools barely functioned. In 2016 she started recording
lessons on her phone against a whiteboard nailed to her living-room wall.

Dars Online grew into a library of 900 video lessons in Dari and Pashto,
covering the national curriculum for grades 7–12. At its peak, the site and
its offline USB distribution reached an estimated 15,000 students. Teachers
in three provinces used the videos in classrooms with a projector and a car
battery.

The problem was never demand — it was bandwidth and money. Hosting costs grew
with every student, mobile data was too expensive for most families, and the
grant that funded the USB program ended in 2019 and was not renewed. Zahra
kept the servers running out of pocket for another year, then made the
hardest call of her life and shut them down.

The videos survive. They were mirrored by volunteers and still circulate on
memory cards through Herat's markets — a library without a building, still
teaching.
