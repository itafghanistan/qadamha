The complete research archive of Pamir Pay — eighteen months of user
research, agent-network economics, fraud playbooks, and the full central
bank licensing correspondence — has been donated to a university library,
where it is available to students and researchers.

The founders' condition was simple: anyone building financial inclusion for
Afghanistan may read everything. "We didn't get to launch," Tamana Faizi
said at the handover. "But whoever tries next starts eighteen months ahead
of where we did. That was the whole point of writing it all down."

The archive includes the final pilot report from Kote Sangi, covering 2,000
users and 140 merchants.
