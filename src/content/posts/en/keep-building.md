People ask me if I regret Aseel Express. Three years, our savings, and it
ended anyway — wasn't it a waste?

Here is what I tell them. Two of our riders now run their own courier
services. Our routing spreadsheets — the ones we argued over at midnight —
still circulate among Kabul delivery startups. Forty families saw, from the
inside, that a business is not magic; it is lists, and patience, and showing
up when it rains.

None of that existed before we tried. A country doesn't learn to build
companies from books. It learns from attempts — including, maybe especially,
the ones that don't survive.

So no, I don't regret it. I regret only the things we didn't write down.
That's why this archive matters: it is the notebook of all of us. Add your
pages to it. And then — keep building.
