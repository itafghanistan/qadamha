Sulaiman and Hadia met at a game jam in 2019 and decided Afghanistan deserved
its own game studio. CodeGate's first title, *Kite Runner's Sky*, put players
on the rooftops of old Kabul during kite-fighting season — art hand-painted
by Hadia from her grandfather's photographs.

The demo won a small regional prize and a publisher expressed interest. The
team grew to six, working from a single room with two gaming PCs shared in
shifts.

The game was 70% complete in August 2021. Within a year, all six team members
were scattered across four countries. Day jobs, asylum paperwork, and grief
took the hours that the game used to fill. The repository is still private,
still 70% complete.

Sulaiman and Hadia haven't deleted it. "Unfinished isn't dead," Hadia says.
"Someday the sky over Kabul will be full of kites again — in the game, and
above it."
